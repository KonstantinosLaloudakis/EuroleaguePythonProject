# Possession DNA: Greek Derby Analysis
## Olympiacos vs Panathinaikos — Euroleague 2025-26

---

|--------|:-:|:-:|

| 2PT Make Rate | **27.1%** | **29.4%** |
| 3PT Make Rate | 12.4% | 10.4% |
| Turnover Rate | 16.1% | **14.3%** |
| FT Make Rate | **17.2%** | 15.3% |

---

## Key Conclusions

### 1. Panathinaikos is the Interior Powerhouse
PAN converts **29.4%** of possessions into 2PT makes vs OLY's 27.1%. They are the more dominant interior team this season, relying on paint scoring and driving to the basket. Their offensive identity is built around high-percentage looks close to the rim.

### 2. Olympiacos is the Better 3-Point Team
OLY hits threes at a **12.4%** possession rate vs PAN's 10.4%. This 2-point gap is significant over 2,000+ possessions — it translates to roughly **40 more made threes** across the season. OLY stretches the floor more effectively and uses the three-ball as a key weapon.

### 3. Olympiacos Draws More Fouls
OLY gets to the free throw line on **17.2%** of possessions compared to PAN's 15.3%. This suggests OLY plays a more aggressive, contact-seeking style — likely driven by Milutinov and Vezenkov attacking the paint and drawing defensive fouls.

### 4. Panathinaikos Takes Better Care of the Ball
PAN turns it over on only **14.3%** of possessions vs OLY's 16.1%. This is a meaningful gap — approximately **40 fewer turnovers** across the season. PAN runs a more controlled, methodical offense with fewer live-ball giveaways.

### 5. Olympiacos is the More Unselfish Team
OLY has a **24.9%** assist rate on field goals vs PAN's 21.7%. Nearly 1 in 4 OLY makes are assisted, meaning they generate more shots through ball movement and team play rather than isolation scoring.

### 6. Possession Origins Tell a Different Story
| Start State | OLY | PAN |
|-------------|:---:|:---:|
| Dead Ball | 32.4% | 30.6% |
| After Made Basket | 29.9% | 29.0% |
| **Defensive Rebound** | 16.8% | **19.6%** |
| **Offensive Rebound** | **13.6%** | 11.7% |
| After Opponent TO | 7.0% | **8.3%** |
| Live-Ball Steal | 0.4% | 0.8% |

- **PAN grabs more defensive boards** (19.6% vs 16.8%), meaning their opponents miss more shots against them — a sign of stronger team defense.
- **OLY gets more offensive rebounds** (13.6% vs 11.7%), giving them more second-chance possessions — a testament to their size advantage (Milutinov, Fall, Hall).
- **PAN forces more turnovers** (8.3% + 0.8% steals vs OLY's 7.0% + 0.4%), creating more transition scoring opportunities.

---

## The Bottom Line

**Olympiacos** plays a **spread-the-floor, draw-contact** style. They shoot more threes, get to the line more often, and move the ball unselfishly. Their weakness is turnovers and giving up defensive boards.

**Panathinaikos** plays a **paint-dominant, controlled** style. They take care of the ball, dominate the defensive glass, and force turnovers to create easy transition looks. Their weakness is perimeter shooting and free throw generation.

In a head-to-head matchup, the key question becomes: **Can OLY's perimeter spacing overcome PAN's interior physicality and ball security?**
